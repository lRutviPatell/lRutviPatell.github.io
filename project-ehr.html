Case Study

EHR Impact on Healthcare Quality

Academic case study on the role of electronic health records in improving care delivery.

Problem

Fragmented patient records and manual workflows reduce efficiency and increase clinical errors. Traditional paper-based systems often lead to delayed diagnosis and treatment errors due to illegible handwriting or missing information.

Approach

Reviewed literature, analyzed clinical workflows, and evaluated EHR-enabled data integration. The study focused on three key areas:

  • Interoperability standards (HL7, FHIR).
  • User Interface usability for clinicians.
  • Data privacy and HIPAA compliance.

Outcome

Findings show improved data accuracy, faster decision-making, and enhanced patient safety. Implementation of integrated EHR systems reduced medication errors by 30% in simulated environments.
